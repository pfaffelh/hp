The Mathematical Institute at the University of Freiburg has been home to the AG Pfaffelhuber since 2008. We like interdisciplinary research with applications in the life sciences, in particular we work in the fields of population genetics, forensic genetics, and neuroscience. Recently, there are also activities of formalizing probability using the Lean Theorem Prover.

The characterization and convergence of stochastic processes can be studied using (so-called) martingale problems. We are working on the development of new convergence results that use this technique. In recent years, mathematicians have been able to formalize their research results using the Lean Theorem Prover. We are working to expand the stochastic section of the corresponding library.
