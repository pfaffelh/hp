Bachelor thesis: The variance of the playing time in Gambler's Ruin (January 2026)

Two players play against each other. Both players stake one Euro per round; player \(A\) (\(B\)) wins with probability \(p\) (\(1-p\)). Starting with bankrolls \(x\) and \(N-x\), the game runs for a random time \(T\) until one player is ruined. The classical result is \(E[T] = x(N-x)\); the aim of the thesis is to compute \(V[T]\). This can be done via generating functions, or somewhat more directly.
